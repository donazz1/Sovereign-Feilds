The Wellness Circuit

Sauna → cold plunge → zen terrace → open farmland. Cedar dry sauna seats four with Harvia heater. Cold plunge chills to 10–15°C. Sliding glass on the full south wall opens onto the farmland. The outdoor zen terrace is completely open to sky with a fieldstone meditation daybed and shallow water basin.

  • Full insulated wall separates wellness from utility — no sound, no smell crossover
  • Independent ventilation — negative pressure on utility side
  • 30 kW solar array on south-facing barn roof — ~75 panels
  • Starlink on barn roof — automatic failover if fiber down

The Pool & Outdoor Circuit

A geometric saltwater pool runs east-west parallel to the house, positioned off the south veranda without interrupting the farmland view beyond. A tanning ledge steps into the shallow end. In-pool LED lighting, automatic cover, and a nearby outdoor shower complete the pool zone. The hot tub sits separately near the veranda fire pit — a distinct destination rather than an afterthought.

  • Geometric pool ~20 × 42 ft · tanning ledge · saltwater system · automatic cover
  • In-pool LED lighting · full automation via Control4/Savant
  • Outdoor shower adjacent to pool
  • Pool house east of pool — changing room, storage, equipment
  • Hot tub separate — near veranda fire pit, not attached to pool
  • Flagstone pool deck throughout · perennial planting beds at edges
  • Pool oriented east-west so farmland view is preserved to the south
  • Granite fountain at kitchen garden patio · separate from pool zone

Outdoor Living Zones

Five distinct outdoor living zones extend south from the house into the landscape — each with its own character and purpose.

  • Covered Veranda — full width of great room south face, tongue-and-groove ceiling, timber posts, integrated lighting, deep enough for dining and lounging
  • Fire Pit Terrace — flagstone, gas fire pit, hot tub nearby, Adirondack seating, views south to open fields and stars
  • Pool Zone — geometric saltwater pool, tanning ledge, pool deck, outdoor shower, pool house to east
  • Kitchen Garden Patio — granite fountain, raised beds, herb garden, casual seating, near mudroom entry
  • Wildflower Meadow — beyond the pool, the landscape transitions from structured to natural — wildflowers, stone walls, hedgerows, open farmland

Landscaping

Structured near the house, natural further out. The landscape transitions gradually from formal perennial gardens at the entry court, through the pool and veranda zone, to wildflower meadow and open farmland beyond.

  • Mature maples along full driveway length · lighted stone entry pillars
  • Perennial gardens at front entry court · lavender beds at all bedroom courtyards
  • Orchard and food garden · wildflower meadow in open fields
  • Stone walls and hedgerows throughout property edges
  • Full property irrigation system · full property landscape lighting with zone control
  • Invisible fence for dogs · flagstone paths connecting all zones
  • Granite fountains at entry court and kitchen garden patio
