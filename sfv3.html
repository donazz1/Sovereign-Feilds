The Pergola

A Douglas fir and black steel pergola with an open lattice trellis roof positioned just off the kitchen garden, east of the mudroom. Completely open on all four sides — the farmland view is visible from every seat. A short flagstone path through the herb garden connects it directly to the kitchen. In early spring the open trellis lets full sun through. By June the vines have filled in creating a dense natural living canopy. By October the leaves drop, opening it back up to sky.

  • Structure — Douglas fir timber posts and beams · black steel lattice trellis roof · no walls, completely open on all four sides · farmland view from every seat
  • Living Canopy — hops or Virginia creeper trained through trellis · bare in spring (open sky dining) · full canopy June–September · brilliant red fall colour · dormant and open through winter
  • Lighting — Lutron-controlled outdoor zone · warm pendant lights hanging from trellis beams · string lights woven through vines · dimmable scenes via Control4 (dinner mode, ambient mode)
  • Ceiling Fans — 4× large black ceiling-mount fans on trellis beams · dual purpose: summer air circulation and evening insect deterrent · Control4 "insect mode" runs all fans at medium speed from app
  • Infrared Heaters — 4–6× ceiling-mount infrared units bolted to trellis beams · heat occupants directly not the air, so wind doesn't steal warmth · extends season into October and from April · Control4 zone on/off from app
  • Seating — one or two long harvest tables · estate dining format · not café-style small tables · seats 10–16 comfortably
  • Floor — flagstone matching house and veranda · low fieldstone border walls optional on north and east sides for wind break without enclosing the space
  • Kitchen Connection — flagstone path through herb garden to mudroom · 20–30 steps from kitchen to table · outdoor kitchen nearby

Smart Home Integration

  • Lutron outdoor lighting zone — pergola pendant and string lights · dimmable · scene-programmable
  • Control4 pergola zone — fans, infrared heaters, and lighting all on single zone · app controllable
  • "Dinner scene" — lights at 60%, heaters on, fans low · one tap from phone
  • "Insect mode" — all fans at medium speed · triggered from Control4 app or voice
  • "Evening wind-down" — lights at 20%, heaters on full, fans off
  • Irrigation drip lines extended from kitchen garden to vine planters at each pergola post

The Greenhouse

A clean black aluminum frame greenhouse — 16×24 ft — anchoring the north end of the kitchen garden zone so it doesn't shade the growing beds. Double-glazed polycarbonate panels for better insulation than glass in Ontario winters. Heated with a small electric unit, plumbed with water, integrated with the property smart system. Big enough to grow seedlings in early spring, overwinter tender plants, and serve as a warm green sanctuary on a February day.

  • 16×24 ft · black aluminum frame · double-glazed polycarbonate panels · gable roof
  • Heated — electric unit with thermostat · integrated with Control4 for remote temperature monitoring
  • Plumbed — cold water line · drip irrigation inside · floor drain
  • Potting bench along one wall · cedar shelving for seedling trays · hanging baskets
  • Automatic roof vents for summer ventilation · screened door entry
  • Positioned at north end of garden zone so it never shades the raised beds
  • Simple and functional — not ornate. Clean lines matching property aesthetic

Kitchen Garden Zone

A dedicated growing zone on the east side of the property, ~150 metres from the house — close enough to walk to with a basket, far enough to feel like its own destination. Stone path leads from the house through the landscape directly to the zone. A small cedar tool shed sits at the entry.

  • Raised Beds — 4–6 cedar raised beds at waist height (~900mm) · no bending · tomatoes, herbs, greens, root vegetables, squash · drip irrigation to each bed
  • Orchard — 6–8 established fruit trees in loose grid · apple, pear, plum, sour cherry · all well-suited to King Township climate · mown grass paths between trees · pollinates naturally
  • Tool Shed — 8×10 ft · cedar board and batten matching property · stores tools, soil, seeds, fertilizers · potting area outside under small overhang
  • Beehives — 2–3 hives at the orchard edge · pollinates all beds and orchard · produces property honey · screened from main paths
  • Irrigation — drip lines to all raised beds · connected to Hunter Pro-HC smart controller · watering schedules set via Control4 app · rain sensor override
  • Stone Path — flagstone path from house east wing through landscape to garden zone · lined with lavender and ornamental grasses · intentional destination walk
  • Herb Garden — dedicated herb bed near tool shed · rosemary, thyme, sage, basil, mint, chives · close to greenhouse for easy access year-round
