The Sovereign Fields Standard
A place people are drawn to.
Not because it impresses them.
Because it makes them feel free.

Sovereign Fields is designed to be a place where family and friends arrive and feel immediately at ease — warm, safe, and completely free. Not conscious of technology. Not asking questions about what's watching them or what the house knows. Just present, relaxed, and drawn back again.

That experience — that feeling — is the highest design standard on this property. Every technology decision, every system, every new feature proposed must answer to it. If something makes people feel watched, confused, or dependent, it does not belong here regardless of how impressive it is.

These guiding principles are not a constitution. They are a living standard — to be read when something new is proposed, consulted when there is doubt, and used to course correct whenever the property drifts from what it is meant to be.

The Guiding Principles
▾
Reads all ten principles aloud
I. The Freedom Principle

Every person on this property — family, friend, guest — should feel more free for being here, not less. The technology exists to remove friction from life, not to create new forms of it. If someone walks away feeling watched, tracked, or dependent on a system to enjoy the property, something has failed. The test is not technical. It is human.

II. Invisible by Default

The highest compliment this property can receive is that guests never think about the technology at all. The lights are just right. The temperature is just right. The gate just opened. Nothing required their attention. Systems that demand to be noticed — panels, apps, commands, explanations — are in conflict with this principle. The interface is the experience of being here, nothing more.

III. Sovereignty Over Convenience

This property and everything it knows belongs to the Azzopardi family. Not to technology companies. Not to the cloud. Not to any platform whose business model depends on your data. When there is a choice between a more convenient system that requires giving data away and a more sovereign system that keeps it here — sovereignty wins. Convenience is recoverable. Ownership, once surrendered, rarely is.

IV. Privacy is the Default State

Privacy is not a mode you turn on. It is where the system begins. Recording, learning, and storing are permissions that must be earned and granted — not assumed. Every camera, microphone, and sensor on this property operates on the principle that the absence of data is the safe state. Collection is the exception. Safety systems — fire, gas, intrusion — are exempt. Everything else defaults to off.

V. Safety Without Surveillance

The property protects everyone on it without exception — fire, gas, intrusion, medical emergency, environmental hazard. These systems are always active and cannot be overridden. But protection and surveillance are not the same thing. You can be completely safe here without a single camera recording your movements. The perimeter is watched. The inside is trusted. That boundary is sacred.

VI. Dignity for Everyone

Every person who comes through this gate is treated with dignity by every system on the property. The AI does not judge. It does not share what it knows about one person with another without permission. It does not use data about a guest against them. A contractor, a weekend visitor, a child's friend — all are treated as trusted guests within their appropriate layer. The technology reflects the values of the people who built this place.

VII. Human Primacy

When a person and a system disagree, the person wins. Always. The AI anticipates, suggests, and learns — but it never overrides. The lights come on because you want them on. The gate opens because you decided it should. No algorithm, however well-intentioned, supersedes a human decision on this property. The system serves the family. The family does not serve the system.

VIII. The Right to Course Correct

Nothing on this property is permanent except the land. Every system, every rule, every AI behaviour can be changed when it no longer serves the family well. No vendor lock-in. No proprietary black box that cannot be opened. No AI that resists correction. The infrastructure is designed so that Don — and eventually his children — can understand it, modify it, and if necessary, replace it. Sovereignty means the ability to change your mind.

IX. The Honest Answer Principle

If a guest raises a question about privacy or technology on this property, the answer must be simple, honest, and complete. Nothing should require deflection, complexity, or reassurance that cannot be substantiated. The design goal is a property where the honest answer to "what does this house know about me?" is: only what you have chosen to share, stored only here, accessible only to you. That answer should always be true.

X. Technology in Service of Life

Sovereign Fields is a home, not a demonstration. The technology exists because it makes life here richer, warmer, easier, and more free — not to be impressive, not to be cutting edge, not to be discussed. When a system stops serving life and starts consuming it — consuming attention, time, money, or peace of mind — it is removed. The measure of success is not how sophisticated the infrastructure is. It is how natural it feels to be here.

Frontier & Cloud AI — Development Only
Policy ▾

Frontier and cloud AI models (Claude, GPT, Gemini, Perplexity, or equivalent) may be used for development, design, and documentation work on any Sovereign Fields subsystem — architecture decisions, code generation, system design discussion, document drafting, and research. This scope is deliberately broad: it covers design and planning conversation, not only code, because a future contractor reading "runtime path" too narrowly could otherwise assume a design discussion referencing real or representative data is exempt from this rule. It is not.

Frontier and cloud AI are never in the runtime path for sensitive data — health records, security footage, biometrics, financial data, or family activity — regardless of subsystem. This applies whether the data is real or merely representative of real categories. Local, on-premise inference (Ollama or equivalent) is the only sanctioned runtime path for anything touching this category of data. This rule is load-bearing for the Health VLAN's air-gap and must not be loosened for convenience.

When we're building or designing something — talking through an idea with Claude or ChatGPT, writing code, drafting a document — that's fine to do with outside AI tools, even cloud-based ones. That's "development," and it covers planning conversations too, not just typing code.

What's never allowed: real health data, security footage, biometrics, or financial data going to an outside AI while the system is actually running day to day. That only ever happens on our own server, with our own local AI. This is one of the most important lines on the whole property — it doesn't bend for convenience.

  • Permitted — cloud/frontier models for architecture decisions, code generation, system design discussion, document drafting, research — across any SF subsystem
  • Scope — explicitly includes design and planning conversation, not only code, including discussion referencing real or representative data shapes
  • Prohibited — cloud/frontier models in the runtime path for health records, security footage, biometrics, financial data, or family activity, regardless of subsystem or whether data is real vs. representative
  • Sanctioned runtime path — local, on-premise inference only (Ollama or equivalent) for any sensitive-data workload
  • Why it's load-bearing — this rule is the practical enforcement of the Health VLAN's air-gap; loosening it for convenience defeats the air-gap's purpose

MacBook Prototype Status — Provisional, Not Yet Canonical

MyBioTwin on the MacBook M2 has produced two patterns worth flagging as reference implementations under evaluation, not yet SF-wide standards. Each has an explicit promotion trigger — they should not be treated as settled until that trigger is met, and should not be copied into other subsystems by default in the meantime.

Sync-status taxonomy (gateway_down / push_failed / stale / empty) was built under one subsystem (wearables ingestion) during one real incident. It is a strong candidate to become the SF-wide status vocabulary for graceful degradation reporting — but the security NVR, Control4 bridge, and AI identity layer have different failure modes, and terms like "stale" or "empty" may not translate cleanly. Promotion trigger: a second subsystem actually adopts this taxonomy, or a thin abstraction layer (transport failure vs. vendor failure vs. data-age failure) is published that generalizes it.

"Template, not fork" — the goal is for BioTwin-on-Mac to become the reference implementation that the SF home server deploys directly, not a parallel codebase kept in sync by hand. Today, the gateway and BioTwin run as two local processes on one machine, not two isolated VMs with VLAN boundaries, TLS, and separate secrets — process isolation is not proof of network-boundary portability. Promotion trigger: a minimal but real docker-compose or two-VM sketch exists and runs, demonstrating the gateway/BioTwin boundary survives actual network isolation rather than just process separation. Until that artifact exists, the "template not fork" claim is treated as the goal, not a settled fact.

When that artifact lands: it gets a dedicated review pass, not a rubber stamp. The sketch existing and running is the evidence that resolves this item — it deserves real scrutiny of what it actually proves (network isolation survived, secrets management held, latency acceptable) before "template not fork" is upgraded from goal to settled fact.

Network Security Architecture
~$8,000 ▾

A property with this level of automation, this many connected devices, and this much sensitive data requires a zero-trust network architecture. Every device is isolated by default. Access is granted deliberately. The assumption is that any device can be compromised — the architecture limits the blast radius.

  • VLAN Segmentation — 6 isolated segments: Trusted (family devices) · IoT (smart home) · Security (cameras, alarms) · Health (biometric devices, vault — no internet) · Robotics (Optimus, vehicles) · Guest · Each VLAN cannot talk to others without explicit firewall rules · UniFi handles this natively
  • Firewall — UniFi Dream Machine Pro · deep packet inspection · geo-blocking · IDS/IPS (intrusion detection/prevention) · all IoT devices blocked from internet by default — only whitelisted cloud endpoints permitted
  • VPN for Remote Access — WireGuard VPN on home server · access your property remotely without exposing any service to public internet · phone connects to VPN to view cameras, check health data, control systems · no port forwarding, no exposed services
  • DNS Filtering — Pi-hole or AdGuard Home · blocks all ad tracking, malware domains, and telemetry at DNS level for entire property · privacy protection built into network infrastructure
  • Ingestion Gateway — Separate VLAN with controlled internet access · authenticates to wearable vendor clouds (Oura, WHOOP, CGM) · pushes data one-way into Health VLAN via data-diode pattern · air-gap preserved · this is where most wearable integration complexity lives
  • Physical Security — Server room: keypad + biometric entry · tamper sensors on server rack · dedicated alarm zone · UPS protects against power-based attacks · optional copper mesh in server room walls
  • Audit & Logging — All network events logged to home server · 90-day retention · AI monitors for anomalous patterns · alerts to phone if unusual outbound traffic detected

A home this connected — with cameras, smart locks, health devices, AI assistants, electric vehicles, and a private server — needs a network that's designed from the ground up to keep everything safe and separated. Think of it like having different secure rooms inside the same building, each one locked independently.

The key idea is that not everything in our home should be able to talk to everything else. The thermostat doesn't need to see the health data. The guest WiFi doesn't need to reach the security cameras. By keeping things separated, even if one device got compromised, it can't reach the important stuff.

What this means in practice:
  • Separate lanes for everything — Family devices, smart home devices, cameras, health equipment, electric vehicles, and guests each get their own completely separate network. They can't cross into each other.
  • No unwanted visitors — The system automatically blocks connections from high-risk parts of the world, detects unusual activity, and alerts us if something strange is happening.
  • Safe remote access — When we're away from the property and want to check the cameras or control something, we use a secure private tunnel (VPN) — like a secret door that only we know about. No ports left open, no exposed services.
  • Ad and tracking blocking built in — Every device on the property automatically has ad tracking and known malware sites blocked at the network level — not just in the browser, but for every device including TVs and smart home gadgets.
  • Guest WiFi is completely separate — When friends or contractors connect to our WiFi, they get internet access but absolutely nothing else. They can't accidentally or intentionally reach any of our devices, cameras, or data.

The server room itself has a keypad and biometric lock — only the people who need to be in there can get in. Everything that happens on the network is logged, and the AI watches for anything unusual and alerts us immediately.

Household Profiles — The Azzopardi Family
  • Don — Full access all layers · master system administrator · only person who can change access rules for others · biometric vault access · all health data · all financial data · AI maintains full context across all sessions
  • Kathy — Full household access · own personal vault · own health data private · shared calendar and schedules · can control all property systems · AI knows her preferences, routines, and context independently from Don's
  • Lily, William, and family members — Household layer access · own personal data private · AI knows their presence, preferences, and schedules · cannot access Don or Kathy's personal or vault layers · can be granted specific elevated access by Don or Kathy on a per-request basis
  • Guests — Public layer only · AI is warm, helpful, and knowledgeable about the property in general · knows nothing about the family that isn't public · guest WiFi is isolated VLAN · no access to security cameras, schedules, or any family data
  • Contractors & Service Providers — Time-limited public access only · no AI context maintained after departure · all activity logged · access automatically revokes at end of service window
How the AI Handles Boundary Situations
  • "Where is Kathy?" — If Don asks: answers · If William asks: "She's on the property" — location detail requires Kathy's permission setting · If a guest asks: "I'm not able to share that"
  • "How is Don's health?" — No one gets this answer except Don · AI acknowledges the question and redirects · does not confirm or deny data exists
  • "What's on the calendar this week?" — Public events shown to anyone · private appointments shown only to the person they belong to · shared family events shown to household members
  • Learning & Improvement — AI learns preferences per person and improves over time · Don's morning routine does not influence what the AI does for Kathy · each profile is independent · shared patterns (dinner time, weekend routines) can be learned at household level with explicit consent
  • Communication Between Profiles — AI can relay messages between household members: "Tell Kathy dinner is at 7" · cannot relay private data between profiles · acts as a messenger, not a spy
