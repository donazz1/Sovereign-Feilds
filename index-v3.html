The Old Barn — A Handsome Landmark in the Landscape

A weathered timber storage barn stands in the open farmland, not far from the house but well clear of the southern views. Room Descriptions
Living Wing · South-Facing

Glass-Walled Great Room Pavilion

The heart of the home — floor-to-ceiling black steel grid windows on three sides, with a direct view south onto the open farmland expanse. A soaring single-story cathedral volume with white-painted shiplap ceiling and white-painted timber beams, bathed in natural light. Statement pendants and recessed lighting illuminate the space at night. Opens fully onto the covered veranda and fire-pit terrace via large pivot doors.

✦ On a clear evening, the sun sets directly into this room across the fields — the entire south-facing glass wall turns amber, and the white cathedral ceiling glows gold from within.
Living Wing · Connected to Great Room

Kitchen & Dining

An open-plan kitchen with a long island, stone counters, and a window above the sink framing a view of the maple driveway approach. The dining area flows directly into the great room, anchored by a statement pendant over a large harvest table seating twelve. A scullery and walk-in pantry tuck behind.

✦ The kitchen window frames the exact view of the maple trees in full autumn colour — every October morning is a painting.
Bedroom Wing · Far End · Most Private

Primary Suite

At the far end of the bedroom corridor — as remote from the living areas as possible. A generous suite with a walk-in dressing room and an en suite bathroom with a freestanding tub positioned to face the garden courtyard. Direct access to its own private garden courtyard via French doors.

✦ The primary courtyard is oriented to catch the morning sun — the first light falls directly onto the stone path and lavender beds just outside your bedroom door.
Bedroom Corridor Wing · Sequential Along Corridor

Bedrooms 2–5

Four additional bedrooms arranged in sequence down the private corridor — each fully isolated acoustically, each with its own en suite bathroom, and each with its own set of black-framed French/garden doors opening onto a private outdoor garden courtyard. No bedroom shares a courtyard.

✦ Each courtyard is planted differently — lavender, meadow grass, herb garden, kitchen garden — so each room has its own sensory identity and micro-world.
Entry Zone · Front-Facing

Entry Hall & Covered Porch

A generous covered entry porch with timber posts and stone foundation anchors the front of the house. The entry hall within is a decompression space — stone floor, built-in bench, coat storage. The sight-line from the front door straight through the house to the south-facing farmland view is immediate and breathtaking.

✦ You open the front door and see straight through to the open farmland horizon — the house frames the land like a viewfinder.
Garage Wing · Angled Toward Driveway

Three-Car Garage & Mud Room

A three-car detached-style garage wing angling toward the paved driveway approach. A mud room with stone floors, built-in storage, and a utility sink connects the garage to the main house. A secondary entry here bypasses the formal front door for everyday arrivals.

✦ The garage wing's angle in the Z-layout creates a sheltered entry court — a private forecourt that feels like a French manor approach.
Outdoor · South Rear · Off Great Room

Covered Veranda & Fire-Pit Terrace

A deep covered veranda runs the full width of the great room's south face, with tongue-and-groove ceiling, timber posts, and integrated lighting. Steps lead down to a flagstone fire-pit gathering terrace set into the lawn. This is the primary outdoor living room of the property.

✦ From the fire-pit at night, looking south, you see only dark open fields and stars — no neighbours, no roads, no light pollution. Just the property and the sky.
Bedroom Wing · Adjacent to Entry Hall

Study / Guest Suite

The bedroom nearest the entry end of the corridor doubles as a study or guest suite — slightly larger than the other bedrooms, with built-in shelving and a window seat. Its private courtyard faces the entry approach side, with a view of the maple driveway through a garden gate.

✦ A window seat built into the deep-silled window frames the maple driveway in perfect symmetry — the room you'll write in every morning.

About These Renders

Original AI-generated concept renderings — Version 2. These photoreal images were generated from a detailed written design brief — a single-story, five-bedroom, ~4,000 sq ft modern farmhouse bungalow compound with an organic Z/zigzag wing arrangement, white board-and-batten siding, a matte black standing-seam metal roof, and a glass-walled great room.

The property is sited on the highest ridge crest of a ~50-acre rural property in Ontario, with the rear of the house facing due south onto open farmland, a maple-lined paved driveway approach, low fieldstone walls, a weathered timber barn in the landscape, and private garden courtyards for each bedroom.

They are fresh, original concept visualizations — not edits or derivatives of any real listing or property photo. Use this viewer to walk the property from every angle, study the design, and iterate the vision before committing to any plans.
